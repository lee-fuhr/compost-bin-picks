Composting at home is one of the simplest ways to cut kitchen waste and create nutrient-rich soil for your garden, but a good bin shouldn’t cost a fortune. The best budget-friendly options keep smells contained, make turning easy, and last for years without rust or cracking. When choosing, focus on capacity (a few gallons for countertop use, around 5-10 gallons for a small household), material (stainless steel or BPA-free plastic with a charcoal filter), and how easy it is to empty and clean.
